# Minimorphism Design System 1.0 (MDS) — Manifesto

## The Design Philosophy of Minimorphism

### IN SEARCH OF A NEW STYLE

We looked around and realized: design had gone to extremes.

*   On the one hand, there was **Flat**, which had become too boring and flat.
*   On the other, there was **Neumorphism**, which looks beautiful in pictures but is unwieldy in reality.

We were looking for a middle ground: a balance between aesthetics and function.  
Thus, **Minimorphism** was born.

This is not just a set of UI kits. It is a philosophy of **Digital Materiality**, built on three pillars: *Physics, Material, and Light*.

---

### I. PHYSICS: EVERYTHING IS AN OBJECT

In minimorphism, there are no "holes" and no flat drawings.  
**Everything is an object.**

We abandoned indentations. In our world, every element—be it a button, an input field, or a card—is a physical object lying on a surface.

*   **Elevated**: All elements protrude above the surface.
*   **Intuition**: The difference between "click" and "enter text" is intuitive—through size and context.

The user sees the structure of the blocks that make up the interface, as if it were a premium construction kit.

---

### II. MATERIALS: RADICAL MONOCHROME

VOLT itself is strict and disciplined. Our palette consists of three noble materials:

1.  **True Black**: The depth of space. Background, active elements, text.
2.  **Pure White**: Energy. Active elements, text, light.
3.  **Anodized Graphite**:  
    Now this is not just another color, and not "gray." It is a premium industrial material. It looks like the matte metal of an expensive device.

We use *Anodized* for secondary elements and substrates. It gives the interface weight, tactility, and an engineered aesthetic.

> **Note to MDS**: We've added other colors (Red, Blue, Green) to the system for the community, but remember: **VOLT is pure monochrome**.

---

### III. SCULPTING WITH LIGHT

No gradients.  
We don't simulate volume with paint. We create it with **light and shadow**.

*   **Technique**: Volume is achieved exclusively through working with *Inner Shadows*, *Inner Glow*, and *Drop Shadows*.
*   **Purity**: We don't smudge colors. We use a solid fill and apply physically based lighting to it.
*   **Result**: The object looks solid, not soft or gelatinous.

---

### IV. SOFT GEOMETRY

The digital world is hard and pixelated. We make it tactile.

*   **Form**: We avoid aggressive angles. Elements resemble polished pebbles or machined metal. They beg to be touched.
*   **Skeleton**: Our display typeface is the steel skeleton of the interface. It contrasts with the soft shapes of the buttons, creating a balance between comfort and technology.

---

### CONCLUSION

We created this style to bring emotion back to digital finance.  
**Minimorphism is when you feel the interface with your fingertips.**

Use these principles. Create interfaces that have weight.
